Mission & Values

Emphasizing the Whole of a Person

Learning is not just an academic endeavor, but a deeply human interaction that involves emotions, values, identities, and relationships. When schools see students only through the narrow lens of test scores, they risk neglecting the social, emotional, and creative aspects that make each kid so unique. By honoring the whole person, educators foster room for growth and safe exploration.

Providing a Platform

Ensuring that students have a platform to voice their opinions, thoughts, and criticisms is important for creating independent thinkers and scholars. A platform fosters self-esteem, agency, and the sense that their perspectives matter. Authentically listening to the voices of each student in the room, each stakeholder in a conversation, enhances the outcome greatly.

Leveraging Student Knowledge

Leveraging student knowledge validates the knowledge and experience that students already bring into the classroom, showing them that learning is a collaborative process; the teacher is not the arbiter of knowledge, but the facilitator of the knowledge-making process. Additionally, utilizing student contextual knowledge makes lessons more relevant and engaging because students can connect new concepts to what might already be familiar to them.

Leading with Compassion

The DMV is currently under occupation, and people are stolen in broad daylight. During these times, it is important to lead with care, to emphasize the needs of those most affected by malevolent forces. Do not despair at daunting times, but lead down the path with care and compassion.
